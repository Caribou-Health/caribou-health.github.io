Public Health Practicum Student

Type
Practicum
Location
Remote – flexible location, potential for hybrid work or team meet-ups in Montréal
Duration
12–16 weeks (flexible to align with academic program requirements)
Time Commitment
Part-time or full-time, 15–40 hours per week

About Caribou

Caribou is a personalized wellness platform addressing critical gaps in the healthcare system by transforming diagnoses into tailored, holistic health plans designed around individual needs.

Role Overview

We are seeking a motivated MSc Public Health student (1+ positions available) to join our team for a practicum placement focused on health promotion, evaluation research, and population health strategy. This role offers hands-on experience applying public health principles to a real-world digital health intervention.

Key Responsibilities

Research & Evaluation:

  • Design and develop evaluation frameworks to assess the effectiveness of the Caribou platform
  • Create survey instruments and study protocols to measure user engagement
  • Conduct literature reviews to inform evidence-based content development
  • Analyze pilot study data and contribute to case study development

Health Promotion & Communications:

  • Develop health promotion content and campaigns
  • Create evidence-based public health reminders and notifications
  • Support development of educational materials
  • Contribute to health marketing strategies

Population Health & Strategy:

  • Identify opportunities to address health equity
  • Research best practices for community health partnerships
  • Collaborate with the full team on product development from a public health lens

Preferred Qualifications

  • Currently enrolled in MSc Public Health, MPH, or related graduate program
  • Strong foundation in public health principles, health promotion theory, and/or program evaluation
  • Experience or coursework in quantitative and/or qualitative research methods
  • Interest in digital health innovation and start-ups
  • Strong written and verbal communication skills

What You'll Gain

  • Hands-on experience applying public health research methods to a real-world digital health intervention
  • Opportunity to design and lead an evaluation study from conception to analysis
  • Exposure to health technology entrepreneurship
  • Mentorship from a multidisciplinary team
  • Portfolio-ready work including research protocols and evaluation frameworks
  • Potential for co-authorship on publications

Clinical Advisor

Type
Part-Time / Advisory
Location
Remote
Time Commitment
3-5 hours per week
Compensation
Volunteer / Stipend (equity discussion upon funding)

About Caribou

Caribou is a personalized wellness platform addressing critical gaps in the healthcare system by transforming diagnoses into tailored, holistic health plans designed around individual needs.

Role Overview

We are seeking a licensed healthcare professional to serve as a Clinical Advisor, providing expert review of the AI-generated care plans, nutrition recommendations, and exercise prescriptions that Caribou delivers to users. This role is essential for ensuring clinical accuracy, patient safety, and alignment with current Canadian clinical practice guidelines.

Key Responsibilities

  • Review and validate AI-generated care plans for clinical accuracy and safety
  • Audit nutrition recommendations against Canada's Food Guide and condition-specific CPGs
  • Evaluate exercise prescriptions for appropriateness, contraindications, and evidence-based standards
  • Flag potential safety concerns or inaccuracies in automated health content
  • Advise on medical disclaimer language and informed consent processes
  • Provide input on condition-specific content (e.g., diabetes management, cardiac rehabilitation)
  • Support regulatory compliance considerations for digital health tools in Canada

Preferred Qualifications

  • Licensed physician (MD), nurse practitioner (NP), registered dietitian (RD), or physiotherapist (PT) in Canada
  • Familiarity with Canadian clinical practice guidelines (Diabetes Canada, Hypertension Canada, etc.)
  • Interest in digital health, AI in healthcare, and patient-facing technology
  • Experience with chronic disease management is an asset
  • Strong attention to detail and commitment to patient safety

What You'll Gain

  • Shape the clinical foundation of an emerging health technology platform
  • Flexible, low-commitment advisory role that fits around clinical practice
  • Opportunity to influence how AI is used responsibly in patient care
  • Potential for equity participation and paid advisory role upon funding
  • Contribute to improving health outcomes for Canadians with chronic conditions
